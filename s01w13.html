// 09.11.21 - Arduino + Machine Learning Exploration: TinyML using Arduino with Complex Gestures to trigger LED with lasting states.

I remember while working on my prototype a few weeks back I was struggling getting the light to stay on while having another action occur. The LEDs would either have to use a delay but when it's on a delay another action cannot occur until the delay is over, or if there wasn't a delay the LEDs would just flash on/off instead of staying on. I asked on the Arduino discord and they shared this really complicated function that I spent two whole days staring at and could not understand how it works? Then when I asked Andreas how would he approach this he mentioned that he would used states to hold the value to keep the LED on while allowing for other actions. Now here comes my adventure into trying to get states to work. A whole bunch of trial and error.

// 10.11.21 - Arduino Exploration: Force Sensitive Resistor Arduino to Arduino Communication to trigger LED Bulbs.

After figuring out that the Arduino Nano's max amount of LEDs is two, I wanted to see if the Arduino Uno Wifi could handle more LEDs but there weren't enough pins to connect them, so I just tried combining them together like this. With the port merging working I started working on my new idea for a prototype. I wanted to use Force Sensitive Resistors that could measure pressure sensitivity (which is one of the forms of sentic modulation that I research for Dissertation) and that could possibly be a form of input for some artefacts down the road so I wanted to create this communication device that measured the amount of pressure you put on the resistor then it would trigger a corresponding light. So a soft touch would be like blue for "calm", a mild touch is green and a hard squeeze could be red for "anger". I also thought the Force Sensitive Resistor was an interesting input to use cause I could attach this behind other materials and calibrate it to work so there are quite a few possibilities for different uses if conductive paint doesn't work out.

// 11.11.21 - Arduino Exploration: Force Sensitive Resistor Arduino to Arduino Communication to trigger LED Bulbs with lasting states..

With the basic idea working, It was annoying that the lights just flashed so once again hello "states". I also wanted to experiment with states so that the devices could have this idea of like "co-presence" like it's not just you sending a signal to someone then they send it back, you could both be touch the sensors at the same time and (ideally) in an artefact I would like the colours to kind of like merge together to sort of represent two people's feelings coming together? That could create something visually interesting as well as encourage more "co-interaction"/"computer mediated remote communication" between people.

Broke the process of writing this part of the code into two parts to make things more digestable and not confuse myself, first working out how to maintain the states on the receivers, before working out how to maintain the states on both receivers and senders.
